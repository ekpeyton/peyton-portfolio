adobeGO

The idea with this design was to create a quick reference guide for Adobe product users. Adobe products have tool hints, which can be kept open while designing. As you select new tools the tool hints transition to that tool and provide a breakdown of what that tool can do, its modifier keys, and shortcuts. It’s a great learning element within the software, but it can be cumbersome to have up on a screen while designing. The mobile guide provides the same information, while building on it with “pro-tips”, but keeps your design screen free for designing. It also meets the needs of users who work on laptops or multiple computers, by being available on a device (their phone) that most users have with them at all times. This concept is supported in the design, by providing individual screens to highlight the functions of each tool. Emphasis was placed on creating memorable and large icons for the tools, since designers will be selecting their visual counterparts when using the software. Sometimes Adobe software can seem overwhelming and serious to new users, so the colors and typefaces used are light, relaxed, and fun.
